Greenroom Design canvas Slice 06 · Dashboard sign-off status v0.1

Sign-off, at a glance.
Three takes on
Mariana’s home row.

The shows list is Mariana’s default screen — the place she lands every morning to know what’s on her plate. Slice 06 adds one new piece of information per row: is the deal signed yet? Three approaches to weaving that signal into the list without breaking the scanning rhythm she already has.

00 / The four states · signed, pending, declined, unsigned-near-showday A / Inline badge · smallest change, hover for detail B / Dedicated column · always-on status with Resend visible C / Triage strip + filter chips · problems first, then the list
00 / 04

The four states

Plus a fifth — not-sent — for deals that exist as drafts but haven’t been sent yet. Same vocabulary across all three variations.
Signed
Agent has signed. The deal is locked. Hover shows agent name + date.
Shown on: upcoming & past
Pending
Link sent, awaiting open or sign. Shows days since sent inline.
Shown on: upcoming
Declined
Agent declined with a comment. Hover shows the comment, click jumps to the deal.
Shown on: upcoming
Unsigned
Only shown if the show is within 48h and still not signed. Pulses to draw the eye.
Shown on: upcoming & ≤48h
Not sent
Deal exists as a draft, no link sent yet. Quietest state — quietly inviting attention.
Shown on: upcoming
Why these five and not more? The spec calls out four for the dashboard, plus the not-sent case the form already enforces. Anything more granular — opened-but-not-signed, partial decline, expired-but-still-pending — lives one level deeper (on the show detail card, slice 07). The dashboard’s job is to surface act now states only.

What changes on the phone
  • 01 Row collapses to two lines: date + days-ago row above, artist name + status chips + money row below. Settlement badge folds into deal-type chip.
  • 02 Resend swaps to a tap-target chip. No more hover-to-reveal — on phone, the action is always visible on pending and unsigned rows. Other rows have the money column instead.
  • 03 Unsigned rows get a faint rose background so the eye catches them in a long scroll. Same pulse animation on the dot.
  • 04 Hover details go to tap details — tapping a signed row drops you into the show detail page where the full signature receipt lives (slice 07).
Why this works. Lightest touch. Mariana’s scanning rhythm is intact — date → artist → deal type → status badge → money. The new sign-off badge slips in next to the settlement badge as just another chip. Resend hides until needed, which keeps the list quiet. Why it might not. Status lives only in the badge, so quick scanning on a busy week relies on color recognition. If Mariana has 20+ pending deals, the badges all look similar and it’s hard to spot what needs attention. The unsigned-within-48h rose pulse is the one explicit alarm.

What changes on the phone
  • 01 Row gains a third line — sign-off status + actions sit below the artist name. Looser vertical rhythm but every row is self-explanatory at a glance.
  • 02 Resend / Send buttons are full-width. Tap-friendly. The action attached to the status is the most important thing per row.

Why this works. Action is attached to status. No hovering, no guessing. Resend, Send for sign-off, and declined comment preview all live next to the badge that triggered them. Most actionable layout. Why it might not. Rows are taller. Mariana’s 25-show scroll becomes a 50-row scroll. On weeks with lots of activity she may want the inline-badge density of variation A.

What changes on the phone
  • 01 Triage cards stack vertically. Single-column shows the urgent + attention items above the fold. Routine pending is collapsed into the filter chips.
  • 02 Filter chips scroll horizontally — like Twitter / Threads tabs. Urgent chip is colored rose to draw the eye toward what’s most actionable.
  • 03 List rows revert to the variation A pattern below the chips. The triage strip carries the cognitive load; the list is just secondary detail.
  • 04 Tapping a filter chip replaces the list with only matching shows. The triage strip stays at top regardless of filter.
Why this works. Most opinionated about Mariana’s workflow. Triage strip surfaces problems first; filter chips let her drill in by state. The list becomes a result set, not the primary navigation. Why it might not. Heavy. On a slow week (zero unsigned, no declines), the triage strip is empty or feels like overhead. Mariana might prefer a quieter list when there’s nothing to triage. Needs a "0 issues" state that feels celebratory rather than null.
04 / 04

Compare

Each variation makes a different bet about what Mariana scans for first.
A · Inline badge
Smallest visual change.
Best for dense weeks — many shows, mostly normal states. Preserves the scan rhythm.

Risk: color-only signal. The rose pulse on unsigned-within-48h is the one explicit alarm.
B · Dedicated column
Always-on actions.
Best for the “every row needs a verb” phase — first 50 deals, before the muscle memory is in place.

Risk: taller rows. 25 shows become a longer scroll.
C · Triage + filter
Problems first.
Best for the “Monday morning” flow — what needs me before I dig into the list?

Risk: overkill on quiet weeks. Needs a graceful zero-state.
Recommendation
Ship a hybrid of A and C: the inline badge from variation A as the row vocabulary (always present on every row), with variation C’s triage-strip appearing at the top of the page only when there’s something to triage — otherwise it’s replaced by a quiet "all caught up" empty state. The filter chips from C live behind a small Filter ↓ button to keep the default surface uncluttered. Variation B’s dedicated column is a useful settings-toggle option for power users on wider screens.

The five badge states (signed, pending, declined, unsigned, not-sent) and the ≤48h rose pulse are the shared backbone.
